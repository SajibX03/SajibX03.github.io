FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions
Here’s a non-exhaustive list of questions I hear often in my office hours, and my typical responses to them.

How did you get into Web Development?

I grew up in Kolkata, a city with a rich history, which played a significant role in my early childhood, sparking my interest in the intersection of technology. After starting my career in web development, I was fortunate to contribute to a couple of projects that integrated with web development. These experiences inspired me to explore more ways to leverage my development skills in addressing the challenges I am passionate about.

How did you get started in Web Developmer ?

I studied Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering in school, and had no idea what “Web Development” was. During my freshman year of college, I took on a website making role in a seed-stage startup and learned some basic development skills on-the-job. From there, I decided to self-teach through what I call “excessive Googling”, and owe a lot of my early growth from there through talented people I met during my first few roles.

I’m new to development. How do I get good at it?

Practice, practice, practice. Early-career Web Development is about getting your reps in: develop a good eye for what looks & feels good, and a sharp instinct for how to approach problem-solving.

What advice would you give to your younger self?

Check in with yourself often on (1) where you’re doing well and (2) what you need to improve on. Developing that self-awareness early will help guide your career. It will teach you what existing strengths you want to polish and what weaknesses you can improve on with the help of inspirational peers, or opportunities for self-learning, or a great manager.
